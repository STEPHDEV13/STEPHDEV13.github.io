Research Interests

My research lies at the intersection of set theory, analytic number theory, and computational mathematics. I am particularly interested in structural approaches to the Riemann Hypothesis, using the foundational principle that "the set of sets is not the same" — the observation that a collection of individually symmetric objects does not inherit their symmetry, and that this failure carries deep arithmetic information.

The Set of Sets Is Not the Same: Divisor Lattice Coherence and Spectral Detection of Riemann Zeta Zeros
Rabarison Mimi Stéphane Thannio — April 2026 — Version 7
We construct a symmetry defect matrix SN(s) from divisor lattices and prove 10 unconditional theorems (A–J): the trace converges to ζ(2s)/ζ(−s) with an explicit pole + oscillatory decomposition; the Frobenius norm satisfies ||SN||2F = Θ(N2−4σ); an exact Hadamard identity links SN(σ) to SN(1−σ) across the critical strip; TN factors as a Hadamard product of rank-2 local matrices T(p)N (matrix Euler product); the rank Q(N) is a topological invariant across all dimensions d ≥ 2; and the Euler product cancellation ratio R∞(α) is strictly decreasing — the zeros of ζ and Möbius cancellation are two faces of the same phenomenon. A zero off the critical line produces anomalous trace growth, detectable through the matrix. Supported by 121 numerical verification scripts.

Proven Results (Version 7)

  • (A) Trace formula: trace(SN(s)) → ζ(2s)/ζ(−s) with pole at s = 1/2
  • (B) Rank independence: rank(SN) = Q(N) ∼ 6N/π² for all s
  • (C) Zero detection: SN(ρ/2) becomes asymptotically singular at each nontrivial zero ρ
  • (D) Frobenius norm: ||SN(σ)||2F = Θ(N2−4σ) unconditionally
  • (E) Rank–Frobenius bound: |trace| = O(√N) for Re(s) > 1/2
  • (F) Ramanujan decomposition: (fs * μ)(d) = μ(d) d3s via Dirichlet convolution
  • (G) Hadamard identity: SN(1−σ) = D−1(SN(σ) ∘ RN)D−1 — exact link across the critical strip
  • (H) Operator norm transfer: ||SN(σ)||op ≤ ||SN(1−σ)||op for σ < 1/2 (RN positive semi-definite)
  • (I) Matrix Euler product: TN = ⨀p T(p)N, rank-2 local factors
  • (J) Dimensional invariance: rank(T(d)N) = Q(N) for all d ≥ 2 — topological invariant
  • NEW — Euler product monotonicity: R∞(α) strictly decreasing — full multiplicativity (α=1) yields maximal cancellation

Numerical Discoveries (121 scripts)

  • Zero detection: σmin(SN(1/4+it)) collapses at t ≈ γk/2
  • Growth detection: a zero off the critical line produces anomalous trace growth Θ(N1−Re(ρ))
  • Nyman–Beurling: primes alone are insufficient (d stagnates at 0.228); composites carry irreducible information
  • Eigenvalue cancellation: 99.7% of eigenvalues cancel at σ = 1/4 — consistent with RH
  • GUE universality: eigenvalue spacing statistics match random matrix theory predictions
  • α-deformation: Fα(s) has no zeros for α < 1; zeros emerge at α = 1 (phase transition)
  • Dimensional extension: rank invariance verified for dimensions d = 2 to 10
  • Farey deviation: δN ∼ N0.477, consistent with RH

Background

With 10 years of study of the Riemann Hypothesis combining mathematics and computer science, I am currently pursuing doctoral research at the École Nationale d'Informatique (ENI) in Fianarantsoa, Madagascar. My approach starts from the set-theoretic principle “the set of sets is not the same” and builds a matrix framework connecting divisor lattices, the Möbius function, and the spectral detection of zeta zeros.
