Research Interests

My research lies at the intersection of algebra, analytic number theory, combinatorics, and ethnomathematics. I pursue two main lines of inquiry:

(1) Riemann Hypothesis & BSD Conjecture. Structural approaches using GCD matrices for L-functions, founded on the principle that “the set of sets is not the same” — a collection of individually symmetric objects does not inherit their symmetry. This framework extends from ζ(s) to elliptic curve L-functions L(E,s), providing a spectral criterion for the analytic rank and encoding all BSD invariants in the trace constant.

(2) Sikidy & Ethnomathematics. Algebraic and combinatorial study of the Malagasy Sikidy divination system. The 16 Sikidy figures naturally form the group GF(2)4, and I investigate the magic squares, error-detecting properties, and finite field structures embedded in this ancestral system.

Magic Squares on the Sorasikidy 16: An Algebraic and Combinatorial Study
Rabarison Mimi Stéphane Thannio — April 2026 — arXiv: math.CO | MSC 2020: 05B15, 05B20, 11T06, 01A13
We study 4×4 magic squares built from the 16 figures of the Malagasy Sikidy divination system. Our central result is the Bit-Balance Theorem: a set of four figures is doubly magic (sum = 30, XOR = 0) if and only if each bit position contains exactly two ones — a binary equilibrium. This yields a canonical decomposition into Balanced Binary Squares (BBS), of which exactly 16 exist. All 2,112 doubly magic squares (528 equivalence classes) are classified as 4-tuples of BBS. Only 144 squares (6.8%) arise from the GF(4) MOLS construction; the remaining 93.2% are sporadic. We further prove that the 192 pandiagonal squares satisfy a half-period complementarity, that the X-magic vector space has dimension 32 over GF(2), and that the traditional Sikidy Judge is restricted to 8 figures (even Hamming weight), revealing an intrinsic error-detection mechanism embedded in the ancestral system.

Sikidy: Key Results

  • Bit-Balance Theorem: doubly magic ⇔ exactly 2 ones per bit position. The magic constant 30 = 2(24−1) is forced by binary equilibrium
  • 16 BBS: exactly 16 Balanced Binary Squares of order 4 exist (8 complementary pairs, 4 symmetric)
  • Decomposition: every doubly magic square = ordered 4-tuple of BBS. Problem reduced from two arithmetics to one combinatorial structure
  • 2,112 doubly magic squares (528 classes mod D4), of which 192 are pandiagonal (48 classes)
  • GF(4) characterization: only 144/2,112 = 6.8% arise from MOLS over GF(4); the other 93.2% are sporadic
  • 140 = 16×35/4: structural proof that XOR-zero quadruples are affine 2-flats of GF(2)4
  • Dimension 32: the X-magic vector space over GF(2) has dimension 32 (232 X-magic matrices)
  • Judge distribution: only 8 of the 16 figures can appear as Judge (even Hamming weight) — intrinsic error detection

Background

With 10 years of study combining mathematics and computer science, I am currently pursuing doctoral research at the École Nationale d'Informatique (ENI) in Fianarantsoa, Madagascar. My work spans two complementary directions: a matrix framework for the Riemann Hypothesis based on divisor lattices and the Möbius function, and an algebraic study of the Malagasy Sikidy system that reveals deep connections between traditional knowledge and modern mathematics (finite fields, combinatorial designs, coding theory). Both lines of research share a common thread: the interplay between binary arithmetic (GF(2)) and classical number theory.
