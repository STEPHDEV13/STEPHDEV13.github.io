Research Interests

My research lies at the intersection of set theory, analytic number theory, and computational mathematics. I am particularly interested in structural approaches to the Riemann Hypothesis, using the foundational principle that "the set of sets is not the same" — the observation that a collection of individually symmetric objects does not inherit their symmetry, and that this failure carries deep arithmetic information.

Publications & Preprints

The Set of Sets Is Not the Same: Divisor Lattice Coherence, Spectral Detection of Zeros, and a New Equivalence of the Riemann Hypothesis
Rabarison Mimi Stéphane Thannio — April 2026
We construct a symmetry defect matrix from divisor lattices and prove that its trace equals ζ(2s)/ζ(−s). We establish a new equivalence: the Riemann Hypothesis holds if and only if this trace is O(N1/2+ε). For Re(s) > 1/2, the bound is proven unconditionally. Numerical experiments confirm that the matrix detects nontrivial zeros of ζ through singular value collapses.

Key Results

  • Trace formula: trace(SN(s)) → ζ(2s)/ζ(−s) with pole at s = 1/2
  • Rank independence: rank(SN) = Q(N) ∼ 6N/π² for all s
  • Zero detection: σmin(SN(1/4+it)) collapses at t ≈ γk/2
  • New equivalence: RH ⇔ |trace(SN)| = O(N1/2+ε) for Re(s) > 0
  • Nyman–Beurling: primes alone are insufficient (d stagnates at 0.228)
